The Grand Three
Performance Model

A proprietary framework for diagnosing and elevating organizational performance, built for leaders who need clarity, not complexity.

01 Three Forces The executive entry point
Strategy
Is the organization clear on where it is going and why? Strategy is the shared operating logic that makes aligned action possible.
Leadership
Do the leaders in place have the capability and influence to drive strategy forward? Leadership is the multiplier on every organizational initiative.
Talent
Is the right talent in the right roles, developed and aligned to performance? Talent is the living infrastructure of strategy.
▼
02 Four Foundations The organizational diagnostic layer
Clarity
Shared direction, defined expectations, and strategic coherence at every tier.
Accountability
Systems and culture that make ownership real, not performative.
People
The human architecture: capability, capacity, and development at scale.
Culture
The environment that sustains or undermines everything else.
▼
03 Five Drivers The leadership behavioral layer
Vision
The clarity of direction leaders bring to every room they enter.
Alignment
How effectively leaders translate strategy into shared purpose.
Decision-Making
The quality and speed of high-stakes choices under pressure.
Influence
The ability to move people and systems without direct authority.
Execution
The discipline between intent and outcome, consistently.

Our Story

Janice Advani
Founder · 54 Grand Advisory

"People don't grow because they're told what to do. They grow because they trust who is guiding them."

For 20+ years, I built strategy, developed leaders, and turned organizational priorities into measurable results as a senior executive at a Fortune 100 company. I worked at the highest levels of a complex, national organization, partnering with leadership to define where the business needed to go, and then building the people, systems, and capabilities to get it there.

At scale, that meant leading enterprise transformation and change management across complex, matrixed organizations. Driving go-to-market strategy and business development that delivered measurable growth. Building sales enablement and field productivity systems that changed how advisors performed in real client conversations. Overseeing operations, process improvement, and end-to-end project management across large-scale initiatives, from planning through execution. Orchestrating cross-functional teams across product, technology, distribution, and HR to launch tools, platforms, and programs that stuck. And using data and AI to make all of it smarter, faster, and more effective. The work was always connected to real business outcomes: retention, revenue, productivity, and growth.

What I do differently is translate complexity into clarity. I work at both ends of the spectrum: defining strategy with leaders and making it practical enough for their teams to execute. Most organizations don't struggle with ideas; they struggle with turning ideas into action. That space, between thinking and doing, between strategy and results, is where I do my best work. Because a plan that can't be acted on isn't a plan at all.

54 Grand Advisory exists because I kept seeing the same pattern: organizations with solid strategies and talented people who still weren't performing. Not because they lacked ambition. It was because something in the gap between strategy and execution was broken. My job, and my passion, is to find that gap and close it.

And I know this world because I lived it. I started my career as a financial advisor, sitting across from clients, building trust, and helping them navigate some of the most important decisions of their lives. That foundation shaped everything that came after. Whether I am working with financial advisors, business owners, or leadership teams inside large organizations, the insight is the same: people perform when they are clear on the direction, trust who is leading them, and have the tools and support to execute. That is what I build, regardless of the industry or the size of the organization.
